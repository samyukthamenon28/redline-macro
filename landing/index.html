// Process

How It Works

01 — SIGNAL

Data Ingestion

Pull 40+ macro indicators from World Bank, IMF, and FRED. GDP growth, credit spreads, current account balances, inflation regimes, debt service ratios. Raw signal, no smoothing.

02 — MODEL

Ensemble Prediction

XGBoost for tabular macro features, LSTM for sequential regime dynamics. Walk-forward cross-validation. Outputs a 0–1 crisis probability, 6–12 months out.

03 — ALERT

SHAP Explainability

SHAP values expose which indicators are driving each country's risk score. Not a black box. Every alert comes with a ranked attribution breakdown.

// Limitations

What This Model Cannot Do

This model is not a crystal ball. It can't predict geopolitical shocks or policy failures it wasn't trained on. It sees macro fragility — not the specific trigger.

A high score means the conditions for a crisis exist. It doesn't mean one will happen, or when. A 0.85 probability in 1997 Thailand was real. A 0.80 in 2019 Germany was not a crisis — it was a slowdown. Context still matters.

Data from emerging markets is often delayed, revised, or incomplete. Some country scores should be read with a wide confidence interval. The model knows this; the UI will tell you when to be skeptical.
